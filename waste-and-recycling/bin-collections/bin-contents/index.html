General waste bin

What you can put in your general waste bin

  • Broken glass, pyrex or china
  • Broken toys
  • Crisp, sweet and biscuit wrappers
  • Empty paint tins
  • Nappies and sanitary products
  • Pet bedding and litter
  • Plant pots
  • Plastic bags and wrappers
  • Polystyrene trays, cups and packaging
  • Toothpaste tubes

What you cannot put in your general waste bin

  • Batteries — place in a tied clear bag on top of any bin on collection day
  • Gas bottles — take to a household recycling centre
  • Electrical appliances
  • Car batteries — take to a household recycling centre
  • Sharps, needles or syringes — use the clinical waste service
Food waste caddy

Liners you can use

You can line your caddy with:

  • Compostable bags
  • Clear plastic or freezer bags
  • Carrier bags
  • Pedal bin liners
  • Salad, bread and cereal wrapping
  • Newspaper or kitchen roll

Do not use black bin liners, rubble sacks, or hard plastic packaging.

What you can put in your food waste caddy

  • All uneaten food and mouldy items
  • Raw and cooked meat and fish, including bones
  • Dairy products
  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Eggs and eggshells
  • Tea bags and coffee grounds
  • Rice, pasta and beans
  • Baked goods
  • Pet food

What you cannot put in your food waste caddy

  • Non-food products
  • Food packaging of any kind
  • Liquids
  • Non-food materials
Mixed recycling bin

How to prepare your recycling

  • Rinse items using washing up water
  • Remove greasy sections from pizza boxes
  • Replace bottle and jar lids
  • Remove polystyrene and bubble wrap from cardboard
  • Keep all recycling loose — do not bag it

What you can put in your mixed recycling bin

  • Plastic bottles
  • Plastic pots, tubs and trays (including black plastic)
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Tins and cans
  • Tin foil and foil trays
  • Aerosols
  • Milk, juice and soup cartons

What you cannot put in your mixed recycling bin

  • Broken glass
  • Plastic bags and cling film
  • Wrappers (crisp packets, sweet wrappers)
  • Plant pots
  • Toys
  • China and household glassware
  • Polystyrene
  • Pet food pouches
  • Electrical items
  • Takeaway cups
  • Coat hangers
  • General waste
  • Vapes

Aylesbury Vale

If you live in Aylesbury Vale, blue-lidded bins accept mixed recycling plus newspaper, magazines, books, envelopes, greeting cards, cardboard and wrapping paper (without foil or glitter).

Paper and cardboard recycling

If you live in Wycombe, Chiltern or South Bucks, we provide boxes and bags to help you sort paper and cardboard for recycling.

What you can put in your paper and cardboard recycling

  • Magazines, newspapers, envelopes and junk mail
  • Shredded paper — seal in an envelope first
  • Greeting cards and cardboard packaging
  • Corrugated cardboard
  • Toilet and kitchen rolls
  • Books, directories and catalogues

What you cannot put in your paper and cardboard recycling

  • Plastic packaging
  • Cartons (juice, soup, milk — put these in the mixed recycling bin)
  • Waxed or laminated paper (crisp tubes, ice cream cartons)
  • Takeaway cups with plastic lining
Garden waste

Garden waste collection is a subscription service. You need to sign up and pay an annual charge to use this service.

Find out about garden waste collections.

Small household electricals

Small electrical items with a plug, battery or charger can be recycled at the kerbside. Place them in a standard carrier bag on top of your recycling bin on collection day.

If you live in Aylesbury Vale, only items smaller than a shoebox are accepted at the kerbside. Larger items should be taken to a household recycling centre or booked as a bulky waste collection.

Batteries

Batteries must be kept separate from all other waste and recycling. Place loose batteries in a small clear bag and leave it on top of any bin on collection day.

Types we collect

  • Household batteries (AA, AAA, C, D, 9V)
  • Mobile phone batteries
  • Laptop batteries
  • Power tool batteries

What we cannot collect

  • Damaged or leaking batteries — take to a household recycling centre
  • Car batteries — take to a household recycling centre
Textiles

Clean and dry clothes and home textiles can be recycled at household recycling centres or at textile banks. Use the Waste Wizard to find your nearest drop-off point.

Kerbside textile collections

  • Chiltern and Wycombe: order textile sacks for kerbside collection
  • South Bucks: place textiles in standard carrier bags beside your bins
  • Aylesbury: no kerbside textile collections — use a recycling centre or textile bank
Vapes

Place vapes in a separate clear bag (not the same bag as batteries) on top of your recycling bin lid on collection day.

Contamination

We will not empty your recycling bins, bags or boxes if they contain the wrong type of material. For example, plastic in a paper bin contaminates the whole load and prevents it being processed.

If your bin contains the wrong items, our crews will attach a red tag to let you know. Remove the incorrect items before your next collection day.

Extra waste and recycling

Your general waste bin must fit with the lid closed. We will not collect bins with the lid left open.

We will collect extra recycling left beside your bin if it is:

  • placed in a reusable container (not loose plastic bags)
  • left neatly beside your usual bins
  • cardboard flattened to less than 1 metre by 1 metre

Excess items that cannot be collected at the kerbside should be taken to a household recycling centre.
